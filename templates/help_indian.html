What is Indian Sign Language?

Indian Sign Language (ISL) is the primary visual language used by the Deaf community in India. It is an integral part of the social, cultural, and educational life of Deaf individuals, allowing them to communicate effectively without relying on spoken language. ISL is not a universal sign language — it is unique to India and reflects the country’s diverse linguistic and cultural heritage.

ISL is used by millions of people across the country, and it has been recognized by the Indian government and various educational institutions. It is an evolving language that is continuously adapting to the changing needs of the Deaf community, with new signs being added as required.
